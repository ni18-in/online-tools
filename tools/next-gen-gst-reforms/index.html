The "Next Gen GST" reforms represent a major simplification of India's tax structure. The previous four-tier system (5%, 12%, 18%, and 28%) has been rationalized into a streamlined two-tier system with primary rates of 5% (for essential goods) and 18% (for standard goods). A new, higher 40% slab has been introduced for luxury and "sin" goods.

This reform aims to reduce the tax burden on the common man, correct the "inverted duty structure" that was affecting businesses, and simplify tax compliance. Many daily essentials, household appliances, and vehicles have seen a significant reduction in their tax rates, bringing relief to households across the country.
